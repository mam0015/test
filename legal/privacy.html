Privacy Policy

Effective 15 July 2026 · Last updated 15 July 2026

Plain-language summary.

Alert Construction collects only the information needed to provide accounts, projects, estimating, team access, security and privacy-safe usage reporting. Usage analytics never include uploaded plans, quote contents, project addresses, notes, search text or estimate values. AI files are sent to the secured AI service only when you ask for an analysis.

1. Purpose and scope

This policy explains how Alert Construction collects, holds, uses and discloses personal information through the Alert Construction web application. It is written to reflect the Australian Privacy Principles (APPs) in the Privacy Act 1988 (Cth), to the extent they apply.

2. Information we may collect and hold

  • Account information, including email address, verification status and authentication records.
  • Workspace information, including company or workspace name, team role, membership status and audit events.
  • Project information entered by users, including client or project names, addresses, tasks, notes, checklists and attachments.
  • Estimating information, including quantities, catalogue rates, renovation answers, property details, comparable sales and saved results.
  • Files deliberately uploaded for AI review, including plans, drawings, images and trade quotes, plus the resulting AI output and human corrections.
  • Security and activity records, such as catalogue changes, role changes, access revocations and project record changes.
  • Limited usage analytics: the tool opened, page path, account identifier, workspace identifier and timestamp. We do not put free text, uploaded files, addresses, notes, quote contents or monetary estimate results into usage analytics.
  • Privacy, support or complaint correspondence you send to us.

Please do not upload sensitive personal information unless it is genuinely necessary and you are authorised to provide it. Remove unnecessary names, phone numbers, signatures and financial details from plans or quotes before upload.

3. How we collect and hold information

We usually collect information directly when you create an account, enter project information, join a workspace, upload a document or use a tool. A team member may also enter information about another person where they have authority to do so. Some project data is stored locally in the browser for offline use and backup. Signed-in workspaces may also be synchronised to the hosted database.

4. Why we collect, use and disclose information

  • To provide accounts, workspace access, estimating tools, project storage, schedules, backups and team synchronisation.
  • To process an AI plan or quote analysis requested by a signed-in user.
  • To maintain catalogue security, permissions, audit history and protection against misuse.
  • To provide support, investigate problems and respond to privacy requests or complaints.
  • To understand which tools are used and improve the product using limited first-party analytics.
  • To comply with law and protect the rights, safety and security of users and Alert Construction.

We do not use uploaded plans, quote contents, project notes or property information for advertising analytics. We do not sell personal information.

5. Service providers and overseas handling

The application uses hosted technology providers for authentication, database hosting, static website delivery and AI processing. These currently include Supabase, GitHub and OpenAI. Information may be processed or stored outside Australia, including in the United States and other locations used by those providers. We take reasonable steps when selecting and configuring providers, but overseas laws and provider arrangements may differ from Australian requirements.

6. AI and automated processing

The AI tools analyse a file only after a signed-in user actively submits it. AI outputs are preliminary assistance, can be wrong and require human checking. Alert Construction does not use an AI result by itself to make a decision that determines a person's legal rights, access to a significant service, credit, insurance or employment. Users must not use the tools for those purposes.

7. Security

Reasonable technical and organisational safeguards include authenticated accounts, server-side token verification, role-based access, Row Level Security, restricted catalogue permissions, audit records and server-side AI credentials. No internet service can guarantee absolute security. Users must protect passwords and Team Codes, remove access when a team member leaves, and report suspected unauthorised access promptly.

8. Retention and deletion

Information is retained while needed to provide the service, maintain legitimate business and audit records, resolve disputes or comply with law. Detailed usage analytics are designed to be removed after 180 days during normal event processing. Local browser data remains on the device until it is deleted, reset, imported over or the browser storage is cleared. When information is no longer required, we will take reasonable steps to delete or de-identify it, subject to backup, audit and legal requirements.

9. Access and correction

You may request access to personal information we hold about you or ask for it to be corrected. We may need to verify your identity. Some account and project information can be viewed or corrected directly in the application.

10. Privacy questions and complaints

Contact the Alert Construction Privacy Contact using the monitored privacy email that must be configured before production. Please describe the issue and the outcome you are seeking. We aim to acknowledge a privacy complaint promptly and respond within 30 days where practicable. If you are not satisfied, you may be able to contact the Office of the Australian Information Commissioner.

11. Changes

We may update this policy as the application, providers or law change. The current version and effective date will remain available here.
